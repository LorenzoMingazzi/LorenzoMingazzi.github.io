Coupled Problems


This section delves into the study of coupled problems, where multiple physical phenomena interact to influence the mechanical behavior of materials or structures. These problems often arise when different processes, such as thermal, fluid, or electrical effects, work together to affect the mechanical response of bodies. Examples include thermo-mechanical, hydro-mechanical, and electro-mechanical coupling, each providing critical insights for solving complex engineering and scientific challenges.
